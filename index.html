About Me

I'm a graduate student in the CoMPLEX program at UCL, working at the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit under the supervision of Peter Latham and Adam Kampff. My PhD research is concerned with studying and building biologically motivated algorithms for learning in recurrent neural networks, as a model of motor learning. More broadly, I am interested in the neural representations and algorithms underlying mammals’ astounding capacity to learn and adapt in seemingly arbitrary novel environments. To attack such questions, I take inspiration from tools and concepts in machine learning to build mechanistic models of the brain that hopefully explain experimental data.

In my past life, I did my undergrad at Johns Hopkins University, where I majored in Cognitive Science and Classical Guitar Performance and spent three years doing research on visual working memory in the Visual Thinking Lab under the supervision of Jonathan Flombaum and Gi Yeul Bae.
